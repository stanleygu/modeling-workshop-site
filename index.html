Modeling Biochemical Pathways: A Short Course
September 11 - 12, 2014

Info

Goal: To understand the essentials of modeling biochemical pathways, including kinetics, motifs, and the principles of model development. 

Topics: Biological networks and motifs, Intro to Python, chemical , enzyme and gene regulatory kinetics, systems dynamics, genetics networks, principles of model development, model exchange in SBML. 

Format: A two day intense course made up of a series of short lectures alternating with longer hands-on exercises, culminating with a project on the afternoon of the second day.

Instructors: Herbert M Sauro (UW, Bioeng) and Joseph Hellerstein (UW, eScience Institute)
Target Audience: Experimental Biologists, Medical Scientists, Biophysicists, Mathematical Biologists and Computational Biologists from advanced undergraduates to senior faculty, who have an interest in developing cell based simulations, or learning how such simulations might help their research. No speci.c programming or  mathematical experience is required, though familiarity with a modeling environment (e.g. Mathematica®, Maple®, Python, Logo or Matlab®) will be helpful.

Fees: NONE
Refreshments : Biscuits and coffee will be provide gratis
Requirements: Bring your own laptop, Mac, Windows or Linux
Textbooks: Heavily discounted copies of:
1. Enzyme Kinetics for Systems Biology and
2. Systems Biology: Introduction to Pathway Modeling
... will be made available (PDF copies gratis)
